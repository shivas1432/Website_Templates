Animals are multicellular eukaryotes whose cells are bound together by collagen. Animals dominate human conceptions of life on Earth because of their size, diversity, abundance, and mobility. The presence of muscles and mobility is one of the primary characteristics of the animal kingdom.

Featured Wildlife

Elephant

With their remarkable intelligence and gentle demeanor, elephants are the largest land animals, revered for their familial bonds and intricate communication methods.

Dolphin

Highly intelligent and social marine mammals, dolphins are known for their playful behavior, remarkable agility, and sophisticated communication through clicks and whistles.

Lion

Majestic and powerful, lions are apex predators known for their distinctive mane and social behavior in prides, making them iconic symbols of strength and royalty in many cultures.

Penguin

Well-adapted to life in icy regions, penguins are flightless birds known for their distinctive waddling gait, strong sense of community, and adept swimming abilities.

Animal Spotlights

Sparrow

The sparrow, a small passerine bird, is renowned for its adaptability and widespread presence across urban and rural habitats globally. These sociable birds often form flocks, displaying intricate social behaviors and distinctive chirping melodies.

Dog

You can introduce your dogs before they meet using scent swapping. One way of doing this is by taking a blanket, toy, or something else with the dogs' scent on it from each dog.

Cat

House the new cat in a separate room for at least seven days with food, water, comfortable bedding, a litter box, and all the necessities.

Horse

The horse is a mammal that people have valued for thousands of years. In the past, people commonly used horses to get from place to place.

More Wildlife

Frog

Any member of a diverse group of short-bodied, tailless amphibians.

Ant

Ants are eusocial insects of the family Formicidae and, along with related wasps and bees, belong to the order Hymenoptera.

Snake

Snakes are limbless reptiles belonging to the suborder Serpentes. There are more than 3000 species of snakes.
